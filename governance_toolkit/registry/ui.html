    Governance mapping

    How this register's fields support the Australian Signals Directorate / cyber.gov.au joint guidance, Careful adoption of agentic AI. Australia's forthcoming national AI standards are expected to require agent traceability, so recording this now is a head start.

    Accountable owner · status · deployment date
    Incremental, deliberate adoption. Adopt agentic AI step by step with a named owner accountable for each deployment, rather than rolling agents out broadly and unmanaged.
    Unique identity · credential scope · permitted actions
    Constrained permissions. Give each agent its own identity and the least privilege it needs, with an explicit, scoped list of actions and systems it may touch.
    Autonomy level · risk tier
    Start with low-risk tasks. Match autonomy to risk — keep higher-autonomy agents to lower-risk work until controls are proven, and escalate review as risk rises.
    Action logging · log location · audit notes · review date
    Logging & monitoring. Log agent actions so behaviour can be traced, reviewed and audited — the foundation for accountability and incident response.
    Traceability status (derived)
    Rolls owner + unique identity + action logging into a single green / amber / red signal — a fast read on whether an agent's actions can be traced to an accountable human.

    Source: cyber.gov.au — Careful adoption of agentic AI
